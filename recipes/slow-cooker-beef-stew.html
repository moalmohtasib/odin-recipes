Slow Cooker Beef Stew

Description

This slow cooker beef stew is the ultimate comfort food! Tender chunks of beef are cooked low and slow with hearty vegetables and savory seasonings. Serve it up with some crusty bread and you have a cozy and delicious meal that's perfect for a chilly day.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 cups potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup frozen peas

Steps

  1. In a shallow dish, season the flour with salt and pepper. Dredge the beef cubes in the flour mixture and shake off any excess.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and brown on all sides, about 5 minutes total.
  3. Transfer the beef to a slow cooker and add the garlic, onion, carrots, celery, diced tomatoes with their juices, beef broth, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Cover and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Add the potatoes and frozen peas to the slow cooker and stir to combine. Cover and continue cooking on low for an additional 1-2 hours, or until the potatoes are tender.
  6. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste, if needed. Serve hot.
